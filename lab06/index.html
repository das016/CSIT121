Lab 06: My First UI/UX Evaluation

This is my first time evaluating the usability of a website while applying the concepts of web design best practices. I have chosen to evaluate the domain mcdaniel.edu because it is the college where I graduated from and I am very curious to find out how user-friendly and accessible it is to the general public.

Usability

In terms of usability, the homepage of McDaniel College is fairly simple and straightforward, which makes it very easy for users to learn the design and accomplish basic tasks the first time they encounter the design. Once I explored the McDaniel College homepage and I became familiar with the design, I was quite efficient and quick at locating specific pieces of information, such as the requirements of certain majors. In addition, I believe that the design is memorable because all of the major links are located at the top of the homepage. When I was looking for the official transcript request form, I realized I had to search for it using the search box at the top of the homepage. Otherwise, it could be quite challenging and time-consuming to locate it, especially for a first-time user. Nontheless, my overall satisfaction level with the homepage is quite high because I think it is very user-friendly and it is fairly straightforward in terms of finding important information as a student and as an alumni.

Audit Score Results According to the Accessibility Checker

The accessibility checker evaluated the homepage of the domain mcdaniel.edu, and the results stated that the homepage for McDaniel College is not compliant and is currently at risk of accessibility lawsuits due to 2 critical issues. The audit score is in fact 73. Websites with a score lower than 90 are at risk of accessibility lawsuits. The Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) offers detailed global standards to ensure compliance of a webpage for people of various disabilities. One of the critical issues involves the contrast between foreground and background colors, which currently does not meet WCAG 2 AA minimum contrast ratio thresholds. The failing element is the selector: div.slab > div.slab__wrapper > div.field > a.

Personally, by looking at Mcdaniel College's homepage, I cannot notice the contrast problem immediately, but if you hover over some of the links at the top of the homepage, a bright yellow box will appear for each link, which can be a bit disturbing to the eyes, especially for someone who has a medical condition.

CRAP Criteria: Contrast, Repetition, Alignment, & Proximity

On the McDaniel College Home Page, I can see various colors on the layout, including: green, white, black, yellow. These colors are repeated throughout the homepage as you scroll down. Perhaps, the hover element with respect to the links at the top of the homepage is what causes a problem in terms of contrast due to the bright yellow color that appears when you hover over each of the six links. All of the buttons to the links have a hover component and an underline component. In terms of alignment, all of the headings, photographs, paragraphs, and links are perfectly aligned in columns and rows due to the Flexbox property. Finally, in terms of proximity, related information on the homepage is grouped together based on the content which is organized into specific headings to guide the viewer in a logical manner. Overall, this homepage uses hierarchical organization because it includes navigational links to major site sections. It seems to follow the "Three Click Rule."

Final Throughts on Lab 06: UI/UX Evaluation

Overall, Module 6 has shown me how to properly analyze a user interface design by focusing on visual elements that directly engage users. It has also made me consider the manner in which users interact with interface elements, such as buttons or sliders. It's important for a webpage to be easy to navigate and it should be consistent across all pages. Most importantly, the user interface should be accessible to users of all abilities.

Although the layout of a web page is very important, the user experience is critical, especially for university websites whose main goals should be to enhance accessibility of specific information to a variety of potential students and to maximize user experience by: serving as a purposeful resource (utility), accommodating the users' needs (usability), presenting information in a desirable way to attract as many potential students as possible, and advertising/promoting their educational brand in order to maximize profits by enrolling as many academically talented students as possible.
